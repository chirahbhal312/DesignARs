AR for Rural Education: Bridging the Gap through UX & Research
🚀 Project Overview

This project explores the potential of Augmented Reality (AR) in transforming rural education in India. It focuses on understanding real-world challenges in resource-constrained schools and designing a user-centered UX framework that makes AR learning accessible, practical, and impactful.

The goal is not just innovation, but feasibility and inclusivity—ensuring that technology truly benefits students and teachers in rural environments.

🎯 Problem Statement

Rural schools in India often face:

Limited access to digital infrastructure
Lack of engaging learning tools
Low student engagement
Insufficient teacher training in technology

This project investigates whether AR can bridge this gap and how it can be implemented effectively through thoughtful UX design.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze the current state of technology in rural schools in India
Evaluate the effectiveness and practicality of AR in education
Design a user-centered UX framework for AR-based learning
Propose realistic strategies to bridge the digital divide
💡 Proposed Solution

The solution is a comprehensive UX framework for AR-based education that includes:

📱 Simple and accessible AR interface design
🌐 Low-bandwidth and offline-friendly solutions
👩‍🏫 Teacher training modules
🎮 Interactive and engaging learning experiences
🧩 Solutions tailored to rural constraints

This framework is validated through research, user insights, and usability testing.

🔍 Research Approach

This project follows a human-centered design approach:

Discover – Understand rural education challenges
Define – Identify key problems and gaps
Ideate – Generate AR-based solutions
Prototype – Design low and high-fidelity prototypes
Test – Validate with users and refine
👥 Target Users
Students in rural schools
Teachers with limited tech exposure
School administrators
Educational stakeholders
